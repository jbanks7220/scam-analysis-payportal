About

Scam Infrastructure Analysis is led by Jamir Banks, a cybersecurity analyst and former U.S. Navy Cryptologic Technician specializing in scam infrastructure mapping and technical threat documentation.

My work focuses on identifying and documenting the technical ecosystems behind online fraud operations — including domain clusters, hosting environments, wallet attribution signals, tooling reuse, and operational patterns.

Background

During military service, I worked in communications signals collection and analysis, producing structured intelligence reports supporting national security operations. That background informs a disciplined, documentation-driven methodology applied to civilian fraud investigations today.

Methodology

All investigations rely on lawful open-source intelligence (OSINT), network analysis techniques, infrastructure correlation, and structured reporting standards. No hacking, unauthorized access, or illegal surveillance methods are used.
